# Wasatch Front / Tooele Valley Land Use Digest
**Period: April 14–28, 2026**

## This Week's Signal
Grantsville is simultaneously rewriting its Annexation Policy Plan (Ordinance 2026-16), signing an MOU with the Inland Port Authority (Res. 2026-26), and entertaining a density upzone at Wellstone Subdivision (R-1-21 to R-1-12) — three moves that, taken together, suggest the city is actively repositioning its growth boundary and absorption capacity. If you have land under option within a mile of current Grantsville limits, your annexation math may change this quarter.

## What Moved
- **Wellstone Subdivision** (Grantsville PC, 4/21) — Four parcels near Pear & Quirk Streets: paired General Plan Amendment (LDR → High Single-Family Density) plus rezone R-1-21 → R-1-12. Density nearly doubles on a lot-size basis; watch whether staff recommends conditions tied to Transportation Master Plan.
- **Grantsville Annexation Policy Plan** (City Council, 4/15) — Ordinance 2026-16 adopting an *updated* plan, paired with a separate General Plan Amendment item on the same agenda. This is the document that governs which unincorporated parcels Grantsville will entertain — read the redline before next cycle.
- **Inland Port Authority MOU** (Grantsville Council, 4/15) — Resolution 2026-26. No excerpt available; the scope and geography of this MOU are the single biggest unknown in this digest and potentially the biggest signal.
- **Copper Cove Phase 1** (Erda PC, 4/15 and again 4/28) — 45-lot residential preliminary plat, applicant Derald Anderson, at the "northeast corner of development." Appearing on two consecutive PC agendas suggests it was continued; reason unclear.
- **Sun Sage Terrace Phases 4–6** (Grantsville Council, 4/15) — Concept plan presentation only, but phases 4–6 implies an established pipeline project expanding. No acreage disclosed in the agenda.
- **Shelton Ross CUP — 519 W Main** (Grantsville PC, 4/21) — Outdoor RV storage in Commercial Shopping Zone, parcel 01-066-0-0033. Low-intensity use but sets a precedent on Main Street frontage.
- **Class C Roads budget amendment** (Grantsville Council, 4/15) — $3.037M capital increase for road department equipment, plus a Vactor truck purchase (Res. 2026-23). Infrastructure capacity spend, not a project, but signals posture.
- **Parks & Transportation Impact Fee updates** (Grantsville PC, 4/21) — CFP, IFFP, and IFA amendments moving together. Impact fees on new development will reset; timing matters if you're pulling permits.

## Pattern Watch
- **Grantsville is stacking growth-framework decisions in a single window.** Annexation Policy Plan + Inland Port MOU + Impact Fee analyses + Transportation Master Plan presentation all landed within seven days. This is not coincidence — it's a city setting the table before the next wave of applications. Anyone with Grantsville-adjacent dirt should treat April–May 2026 as the rulemaking window.
- **Density pressure is showing up in the existing footprint, not just the edges.** The Wellstone GPA/rezone is an infill upzone, not a greenfield annex. Combined with the Annexation Plan update, Grantsville appears to be working both levers at once.
- **Erda remains quiet by comparison.** One active subdivision (Copper Cove) and a dispatch interlocal. The contrast with Grantsville's agenda volume is itself a signal about where staff bandwidth and developer attention are concentrated.
