# Wasatch Front / Tooele Valley Land Use Digest
**Period: April 14 – April 28, 2026**

## This Week's Signal
Grantsville is running two simultaneous density-up rezones on its east and west flanks — Wellstone (R-1-21 → R-1-12 near Pear/Quirk) and Orion (~800 East Main) — both moving Planning Commission to Council inside two weeks, alongside an Annexation Policy Plan amendment and a comprehensive Land Use Code rewrite touching ten chapters. If you hold or are chasing dirt inside or adjacent to Grantsville city limits, the entitlement rulebook and the density floor are both shifting at once.

## What Moved
- **Wellstone Subdivision rezone (Grantsville)** — R-1-21 to R-1-12 near Pear St / Quirk St; cleared Planning 4/21, on Council 5/6. Note the agenda inconsistency: Council packet calls it "Wellington" while Planning called it "Wellstone" — same four parcels, worth confirming which name is on the recorded ordinance.
- **Orion Subdivision (Grantsville)** — General Plan amendment + rezone on four parcels at ~800 East Main; concept just emerged 5/5. Pairs with the Wellstone push as a second density bump on the same cycle.
- **Grantsville Land Use & Management Code rewrite** — Council 5/6 takes amendments to Chapters 4, 6, 7, 8, 9, 14, 15, 16, 20, and 21 in one motion. That is not housekeeping; that's a structural rewrite. Combined with separate Section 4.5 Lot Standards/Street Frontage amendments at Planning 5/5.
- **Grantsville Annexation Policy Plan amendment** — Adopted 4/15. Sets the perimeter rules for everything coming next; should be read before any unincorporated Tooele Valley acquisition.
- **Copper Cove Phase 1 (Erda)** — 45-lot preliminary plat heard twice (4/15 and 4/28) by Erda Planning. First substantive residential subdivision activity in Erda this cycle.
- **Erda disconnection petitions** — Two parcels petitioning to disconnect from Erda city limits, on Council 5/14. Disconnections are rare and usually signal either a tax/service dispute or an attempt to reposition for county-level entitlement.
- **Brandon Park (Eagle Mountain)** — ~216-acre Future Land Use + Zoning Map amendment cleared Planning 4/14, Council 5/5. Largest single rezone footprint in the period.
- **Sunset Mountain Properties rezone (Eagle Mountain)** — Agriculture to Light Manufacturing/Distribution on two parcels. Industrial conversion in a residential-dominant city is the watch item.
- **Cedar Hollow Holdings annexation (Lehi)** — 15.31 acres at 400 S Center Street, Council 4/28. Paired with separate Slay Holdings annexation (~4 acres at 2300 W 300 S) same night.
- **Vineyard policy package (4/14)** — Five resolutions adopted in one night: vesting control, mandatory development agreements for master-planned projects, infrastructure phasing, utility capacity verification, and independent appeal authority. Vineyard is tightening the screws on large developers; expect longer entitlement timelines and more pre-app friction.

## Pattern Watch
- **Grantsville is in a regulatory reset.** Annexation Policy Plan + ten-chapter LUMC rewrite + Section 4.5 lot standards + two density-up rezones + Capital Facilities/Impact Fee amendments — all inside three weeks. Anyone with a deal under contract in Grantsville should assume the rules at closing won't be the rules at application.
- **Vineyard and Grantsville are moving in opposite directions on developer leverage.** Vineyard is codifying mandatory development agreements and vesting controls (raising the bar). Grantsville is loosening lot standards and approving smaller-lot rezones (lowering it). Capital flowing out of Vineyard on friction may have a natural home in Tooele Valley.
- **Eagle Mountain is rezoning at scale (216 ac Brandon Park) while simultaneously amending its Concept Plan chapter (16.15) and WUI setback definitions.** Watch whether the code amendments narrow or expand what Brandon Park can ultimately deliver.
